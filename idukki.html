Idukki - The Spice Garden Of Kerala


Idukki is a district in the Indian state of Kerala. It was constituted on 26 January 1972, by splitting the district of Kottayam into two parts.[5] Its division was previously headquartered at Kottayam city, but moved to Kuyilimala near Painavu and Cheruthoni in June 1976. Idukki district lies amid the Cardamom Hills of Western Ghats in Kerala.

Popular Destinations


Chinnar Wildlife Sanctuary

For Chinnar Wildlife Sanctuary, being located in the rain-shadow region of the Western Ghats, Chinnar, missing the generous monsoon in June-July and gets its meager share of showers only around November. But the climate creates unique ecosystem at the Sanctuary and attracting a lot of nature lovers.

Kulamavu Dam

Kulamavu is best known for being one of the three dams of the Idukki Reservoir Project .If you're a hiking or trekking junkie, Kulamavu is a hill-station that offers the perfect terrain for an adrenaline kick. With some amazing trekking trails, the place is also adorned with equally enchanting surroundings.

Eravikulam National Park

Eravikulam National Park is the first national park in Kerala. Eravikulam National Park is administered by the Kerala Department of Forests and Wildlife. It is one the place where you can witness the mass flowering of Neelakurinji flowers.The star attraction here is the endangered Nilgiri Tahr.
